Alina engages with the practice of architecture and urban design in an open and collaborative way. Alina approaches her work with curiosity and relishes problem-solving and learning. Currently broadening her skill-set through further study in the field of Software Engineering, Alina is positioned to contribute in the fields of both design and tech, or the intersection thereof.

Alina has completed a Masters of Architecture from The University of Technology, Sydney. She has also worked as a researcher and educator in Architecture and Urban Design Programs at the Architectural Association, the Bartlett School of Architecture and The University of Technology, Sydney. Her research has investigated conditions of urbanism and typology through the use of digital technology. Her recent work in practice has focused on collective housing within the urban context. She also holds a Bachelor of Design from The University of Sydney.

Alina is an experienced project leader with a demonstrated history working on urban regeneration, housing, education, commercial and mixed-use projects. She has worked on large-scale, multi-disciplinary and complex projects across the UK, Europe, Asia, Australia and New Zealand with an expertise at working across scales in both urban design and architecture. She brings a broad perspective on urban challenges, emerging technologies, governance models and policy. Alina has worked in practice in London for Studio Woodroffe Papa and in Sydney for Woods Bagot.


With a strong interest in computation and technology, her last role involved championing Building Information Modelling (BIM) processes and standards. Alina regularly works with scripting and visual programming to restructure how architectural practices can work internally on projects and deliver services to clients. Building on this interest, Alina has recently taken a career break to undertake learning in the field of software engineering developing skills in languages and frameworks including Python, Django, SQL.
