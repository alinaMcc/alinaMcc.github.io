With extensive experience working on complex mixed use projects across the UK, Europe, Asia, Australia and New Zealand, Alina is an expert at working across scales in both urban design and architecture. She brings a broad perspective on urban challenges, emerging technologies, housing typologies and policy. Alina has worked in practice in London for Studio Woodroffe Papa and in Sydney for Woods Bagot .

She has also worked as a researcher and educator at the Architectural Association, the Bartlett School of Architecture and the University of Technology, Sydney. Her research has investigated conditions of urbanism and typology through the use of digital technology. Her recent work in practice has focused on collective housing within the urban context.

Alina has had work exhibited at Venice Biennale 2010, Prague Quadrennial 2011, Object Gallery Surry Hills and DAB LAB Research Gallery; and has had projects published in AA Files, Architectural Design: Post Traumatic Urbanism, MAS Context Journal, Architectural Review and Architecture Australia. Alina has completed a Masters of Architecture from the University of Technology, Sydney. She also holds a Bachelor of Design from the University of Sydney.

This is a repository of her academic work.
